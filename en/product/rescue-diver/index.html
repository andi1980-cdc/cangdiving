Why Take the Rescue Diver Course?

Watch this short intro to get a feel for what rescue training is all about – serious skills, real scenarios, and a lot of team spirit.

Learn to Prevent, React and Rescue

The Rescue Diver Course is one of the most rewarding and empowering certifications in scuba diving. In 3 days, you'll learn to manage emergencies, perform search and rescue, and help others stay safe – while building your own confidence underwater.

This course is recommended for every diver and required for becoming a Divemaster or Master Scuba Diver.

→ Learn how the Rescue Course works – Step by Step

What You Will Learn

  • Recognize diver stress and prevent accidents
  • Conduct in-water and surface rescues
  • Respond to panicked or unresponsive divers
  • Organize search & recovery operations
  • Use teamwork and communication under stress

Bonus: CPR Inspiration with Vinnie Jones

Learn how effective CPR can be – and why it matters. Even tough guys can save lives!

Course Schedule

  • Day 1: Meet your instructor, gear setup, review theory, pool rescue skills
  • Day 2: 2 rescue scenario dives (max. 60 mins or 50 bar limit)
  • Day 3: 2 final rescue scenario dives + skills assessment

Junior Rescue Divers (12–15) max. depth: 21m – parental consent required

Who Can Join?

  • Minimum age: 15 (PADI) / 12 (Junior Rescue, SDI)
  • Advanced Open Water certification (or equivalent)
  • Valid First Aid/CPR certificate (max. 12 months old)
  • Online theory must be completed before course start
  • Good fitness and strong swimming ability

What's Included

  • Pool training + online theory (SDI or PADI)

FAQ – Your Questions Answered

Do I need First Aid training before the Rescue Course?

Yes – a valid CPR/First Aid certificate (max. 12 months old) is required. If you don't have one yet, you can join our First Aid Course before or alongside Rescue training.

How difficult is the Rescue Course?

It's challenging but very rewarding! You'll practice realistic scenarios and build real rescue skills – all in a supportive team setting.

How many dives are included?

You'll do 4 open water rescue scenario dives, plus pool training and lots of hands-on practice above water.

Is this course needed to become a Divemaster?

Yes – Rescue Diver is required for both the Divemaster Course and Master Scuba Diver rating.

Can I take this course as a junior diver?

Yes – from age 12 (SDI Junior Rescue). PADI requires 15+. For 12–15 year olds, the max depth is 21 meters with parental consent.
