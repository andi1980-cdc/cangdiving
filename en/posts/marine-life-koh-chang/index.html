Discover the Marine Life of Koh Chang – A Diver's Underwater Paradise

Koh Chang's marine life is as vibrant and diverse as the island itself. Nestled within the Mu Ko Chang National Park, the coral reefs and underwater ecosystems around the island are home to hundreds of species – from tiny nudibranchs to majestic whale sharks.

Whether you're a beginner diver, advanced explorer, or snorkeler, the crystal-clear waters of Koh Chang offer unforgettable encounters with tropical marine creatures. Expect to meet colorful reef fish like angelfish, parrotfish, and butterflyfish, alongside fascinating macro life such as seahorses and ghost pipefish. For wreck diving fans, the HTMS Chang is not only Thailand's largest shipwreck but also a thriving artificial reef where schools of barracudas, giant groupers, and even turtles regularly appear.

Our Marine Life category dives deep into the species you're most likely to encounter while exploring Koh Chang's dive sites. With warm waters and year-round biodiversity, Koh Chang is the perfect destination to experience Thailand's marine richness in a relaxed, crowd-free environment.
