The Green Sea Turtle (Chelonia mydas) is one of the most fascinating marine creatures frequently spotted in Koh Chang. The best places to see them include HTMS Chang Wreck, Hin Raab South, Blueberry Hill, and Hin Sam Sao.

1. Characteristics & Biology

  • Scientific Name: Chelonia mydas
  • Size: Up to 1.5 meters in shell length
  • Weight: Between 100 and 180 kg
  • Lifespan: Up to 80 years

3. Diet & Feeding Habits

Green Sea Turtles are primarily herbivores. Their diet includes:

  • Seagrass
  • Algae
  • Occasionally jellyfish or small fish

4. Threats & Conservation

Green Sea Turtles are threatened due to:

  • Plastic pollution
  • Entanglement in fishing nets
  • Climate change affecting their nesting sites
