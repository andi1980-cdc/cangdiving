How to Become a Master Scuba Diver – Step by Step (2025, Koh Chang)

What’s This Program About?

The Master Scuba Diver (MSD) rating is the highest level in recreational diving – a recognition of your experience, skills, and passion. At Chang Diving, we guide you step by step through your journey – whether you’re training under PADI or SDI.

MSD is not a single course, but a level you reach by completing required certifications and logged dives. We help you get there with flexible training plans and discounted packages.

Who Can Join the MSD Program?

  • Minimum age: 18 years (PADI/SDI) or 12+ for PADI Junior MSD
  • PADI path: Requires Open Water, Advanced Open Water, Rescue Diver, 5 specialties
  • SDI path: Requires Open Water, Rescue Diver, 4 specialties
  • 50+ logged dives
  • All courses must match the agency (PADI or SDI)

Your Step-by-Step Plan

Here’s how we help you qualify as a Master Scuba Diver:

  • We review your existing certifications and logged dives
  • We create a custom course plan (with discounts if needed)
  • You complete the required specialties and rescue training
  • You log 50+ dives (we can add dive packs if needed)
  • We submit your MSD application to PADI or SDI

Master Scuba Diver – FAQs

Can I combine PADI and SDI certifications?

No – each agency requires all courses to be completed within their own system.

Can I count previous dives and courses?

Yes – we’ll check your logbook and certifications and credit what qualifies.

How long does it take?

Typically 7–12 days depending on course availability and weather.

Is MSD a professional level?

No – it’s the highest non-professional level. For pro training, start the Divemaster Course.

Important Safety Notice:
Please do not use cannabis before or during your training dives. Diving requires full awareness and responsibility.
