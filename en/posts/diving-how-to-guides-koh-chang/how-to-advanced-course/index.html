What Depths Will You Be Certified For?

In recreational diving, your depth limits depend on your certification level. After completing the Advanced Open Water Diver (AOWD) course, you‚Äôll be certified to dive to:

  • Advanced Open Water Diver (AOWD): Max. 30 m ‚Äì worldwide recognition

Here‚Äôs how AOWD compares to other common certifications:

  • Scuba Diver (SD): Max. 12 m (must dive with a pro)
  • Open Water Diver (OWD): Max. 18 m
  • Advanced Open Water Diver (AOWD): Max. 30 m
  • Deep Diver Specialty (DD): Max. 40 m ‚Äì this is the absolute depth limit for recreational diving, even for dive professionals.
  • Technical Diving (TDI, etc.): Certified technical divers may go beyond 40 meters ‚Äì in structured 5-meter increments based on their training level:
    • Decompression Procedures Diver (DPD): Max. 45 m
    • Advanced Nitrox Diver (AND): Max. 50 m
    • Extended Range Diver (ERD): Max. 55 m
    These dives require special certification, planning, and equipment.
