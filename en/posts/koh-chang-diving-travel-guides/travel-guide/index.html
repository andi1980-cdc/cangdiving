Koh Chang – Your Ultimate Guide to Thailand’s Paradise Island

Koh Chang, Thailand’s second-largest island, is a tropical paradise with breathtaking nature, white sandy beaches, and lush jungle. This island has everything your heart desires – from relaxing beach days to thrilling underwater adventures. In this guide, you’ll learn everything you need to know about Koh Chang!

1. About Koh Chang

  • Thailand’s second-largest island: Koh Chang belongs to Trat province and is the second-largest island in Thailand after Phuket.
  • Why it’s called the “Elephant Island”: The name “Chang” means “elephant” in Thai, due to the island's shape resembling an elephant's back.
  • A mix of jungle and dream beaches: Koh Chang offers a perfect combination of tropical rainforest and beautiful beaches – ideal for nature lovers and adventurers.

2. Getting to Koh Chang

  • Nearest airports: The closest airport is in Trat. Bangkok Airways operates daily flights from Bangkok.
  • Ferries and transfers: Minibus transfers from Trat airport take visitors to the ferry and onward to the beaches of Koh Chang. Alternatively, buses and private taxis run directly from Bangkok to the island. More details on Getting to Koh Chang.

3. Best Time to Visit Koh Chang

  • When is the best weather? The high season runs from November to April, offering sunny weather and calm seas.
  • Rainy season vs. dry season: The rainy season lasts from May to October, but fewer tourists and lower prices make it an attractive option. Surprisingly, diving conditions can still be excellent! Read more in our Weather Guide.

4. The Best Beaches on Koh Chang’s West Coast

  • Klong Son: A beautiful and secluded area, home to the exclusive Siam Royal View Resort and condominiums. Perfect for those seeking tranquility, but not ideal for travelers without their own transportation.
  • White Sand Beach: The most famous and busiest beach on Koh Chang. You’ll find numerous resorts, restaurants, and bars – perfect for those looking for a mix of relaxation and lively nightlife.
  • Pearl Beach: A small, mostly rocky beach with a few resorts. There are some restaurants and bars, making it a great spot for those wanting to escape the main tourist areas.
  • Klong Prao Beach: A long, quiet sandy beach with hotels, hostels, and resorts for all budgets. Our boat is anchored between Klong Prao Resort and Flora I Talay Resort – all our dive trips start from here!
  • Lonely Beach: A backpacker’s paradise with a relaxed nightlife scene. You’ll find affordable accommodations, beach bars, and a laid-back vibe – ideal for young travelers and party lovers.
  • Bailan Beach: A peaceful and relaxed area with only a few resorts. Small local restaurants and a tranquil atmosphere make this beach perfect for those looking to escape the crowds.
  • Bang Bao: A charming fishing village with the main pier in the south of Koh Chang. Many boat trips, including diving and snorkeling tours, depart from here. The restaurants on the pier serve freshly caught seafood – a must-visit for food lovers.
  • Klong Kloy Beach: A small, idyllic beach with cozy bungalows and restaurants right on the water. From here, you can enjoy stunning views of Bang Bao Bay and beautiful sunsets.

5. The Best Spots on Koh Chang’s East Coast

  • Dan Mai: The island’s official administrative center, home to the main police station, hospital, and all major government offices. There aren’t many tourist attractions here, but it serves as Koh Chang’s administrative hub.
  • Salak Khok: A small, authentic fishing village with traditional stilt houses. Known for its excellent seafood restaurants and scenic mangrove forests, which can be explored by boat.
  • Long Beach: A remote, stunning white sandy beach with a relaxed, alternative atmosphere. You’ll find charming hippie-style bungalows right on the beach – perfect for those who want to immerse themselves in nature.
  • Koh Chang Memorial: A war memorial commemorating a historic battle between the Thai and French navies in 1941. A significant site for Koh Chang’s history.
  • Salak Phet: The largest fishing village on the east coast, featuring a large pier, traditional stilt houses, and several excellent seafood restaurants. Nearby, you’ll find a marina and yacht club, attracting sailors and boat travelers.

6. Top Activities on Koh Chang

  • Diving & Snorkeling: Discover stunning coral reefs and the famous HTMS Chang wreck.
  • Jungle Trekking: Explore the island’s rainforests on guided tours.
  • Kayaking & Island Hopping: Rent a kayak or join a boat trip to explore hidden beaches.
  • Elephant Camps: Visit ethical elephant sanctuaries committed to animal protection.

7. Why Diving in Koh Chang is a Must

  • Best dive sites: The HTMS Chang is Thailand’s largest shipwreck and a must for divers.
  • Marine biodiversity: From whale sharks to seahorses – Koh Chang’s underwater world is stunning.
  • Perfect for beginners and pros: Whether you're getting your SDI/PADI Open Water certification or planning technical dives, Koh Chang has something for everyone.

8. Food & Nightlife on Koh Chang

  • Best restaurants: Enjoy fresh seafood at beachside spots or authentic Thai dishes at local markets.
  • Where to party: Lonely Beach is known for its vibrant nightlife, while White Sand Beach offers stylish bars and cocktail lounges.

9. Tips for Your Koh Chang Stay

  • ATMs & Payments: Credit cards are accepted in most hotels, restaurants, and dive shops, but cash is always useful. ATMs can be found every 100 meters in major tourist areas, but some remote locations have none – plan accordingly.
  • Getting Around: - **Mopeds:** The best way to get around – no parking issues, and you can explore the island freely. - **Cars:** Great for families or groups, especially if you want to visit remote beaches. - **Songthaews (shared taxis):** Run regularly during the day but can be expensive for late-night trips or remote areas.
  • Accommodation on Koh Chang:
    • Budget: Backpacker hostels in Lonely Beach – ideal for young travelers and partygoers.
    • Mid-range: Resorts in Klong Prao – a perfect mix of comfort and nature.
    • Luxury: Private villas and 5-star hotels in White Sand Beach and Klong Son – for a truly exclusive stay.
